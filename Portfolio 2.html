DATA, INFORMATION, AND INFORMATION SYSTEMS

DATA

The data is independent of the information.

Data or raw data is not enough to make a decision.

Data on a computer is stored as binary data, where every file consists of a series of 1s and 0s called bits.

Data refers to raw facts that have no specific meaning.

The word “data” is derived from the Latin word “datum”, which means “something that is given”.



INFORMATION

It is the set of data that has been organized for direct utilization of mankind, as information helps human beings in their decision-making process.

Information gives meaning and increases the accuracy of the data.

Information does not require analysis and interpretation, it only requires understanding.

Information describes text that's informative to the individual reading it or the computer processing it.



INFORMATION SYSTEMS

Information systems is an academic discipline of the complementary networks of hardware, software, users and business processes that organizations use to collect, filter, process, create and distribute data. Information system, an integrated set of components for collecting, storing, and processing data and for providing information, knowledge, and digital products.
